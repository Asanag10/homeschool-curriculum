Reading & Phonics

45 min/day

Learning Objectives

  • Recognize and name all uppercase and lowercase letters
  • Learn letter sounds and simple phonics patterns
  • Blend sounds to read CVC words (cat, dog, sun)
  • Read 25+ sight words (the, and, I, see, can)
  • Understand that print carries meaning
  • Follow words from left to right, top to bottom

Resources Used

Starfall ABCs (Free) PBS Kids - Super Why! Library Picture Books Phonics Worksheets Letter Tracing Pages

Sample Week 1: Letter A (FREE PREVIEW)

Monday: Introduction to Letter A
  • Watch Starfall Letter A video
  • Sing "A is for Apple" song
  • Find objects around the house that start with A
  • Practice writing uppercase A with finger in sand/shaving cream
Tuesday: Letter A Sound Practice
  • Review letter A shape and sound
  • Read "The Apple Pie Tree" (library book)
  • Sort picture cards: A sound vs. other sounds
  • Color pictures of A words (apple, ant, alligator)
Wednesday: Writing Letter A
  • Trace letter A with finger, then crayon
  • Practice on whiteboard or paper
  • Make letter A with playdough
  • PBS Kids Super Why episode featuring letter A
Thursday: Letter A in Words
  • Build simple words: at, an, am
  • Read "Cat in the Hat" focusing on 'at' words
  • Create an "A Book" - draw/paste A pictures
  • Play "I Spy" with A words
Friday: Letter A Review & Assessment
  • Letter A scavenger hunt in books
  • Write letter A from memory
  • Tell a story using A words
  • Celebrate with apple snack and apple stamping art

Spelling & Word Building

15 min/day

Learning Objectives

  • Spell simple CVC words (cat, dog, sun)
  • Learn sight word spellings (the, and, I, see)
  • Understand that letters make words
  • Practice writing simple words from memory
  • Build word families (-at, -an, -it, -og)
  • Recognize spelling patterns

Weekly Word Lists Include

📝 CVC words (cat, bat, rat, hat)
📝 Sight words (I, see, the, a, to)
📝 Color words (red, blue, green)
📝 Number words (one, two, three)
Texas Requirement: Spelling is one of the five required subjects for Texas homeschoolers. Our kindergarten curriculum introduces foundational spelling skills through phonics-based instruction.

Resources Used

Starfall Word Building Weekly Word Lists Spelling Practice Pages

Mathematics

30 min/day

Learning Objectives

  • Count to 100 by ones and tens
  • Write numbers 0-20
  • Understand more/less, bigger/smaller concepts
  • Add and subtract within 10 using objects
  • Recognize and create simple patterns (AB, ABC)
  • Identify basic shapes and their properties
  • Measure using non-standard units (blocks, hands)

Resources Used

Khan Academy Kids PBS Kids - Peg + Cat Counting Songs (YouTube) Math Manipulative Guides Number Worksheets

Sample Week 1: Numbers 1-5 (FREE PREVIEW)

Monday: Introduction to Number 1
  • Count to 10 together with fingers
  • Focus on number 1: "One is special, it's alone"
  • Find one object in each room
  • Practice writing number 1
  • Khan Academy Kids: Number 1 activities
Tuesday: Number 2
  • Count pairs: eyes, hands, feet, shoes
  • Make groups of 2 with toys or snacks
  • Practice writing number 2
  • Read "Two of Everything" book
Wednesday: Number 3
  • Count sets of 3 (triangle sides, tricycle wheels)
  • Build towers of 3 blocks
  • Practice writing number 3
  • Watch Peg + Cat episode about counting to 3
Thursday: Numbers 4 & 5
  • Count fingers on one hand (5), table legs (4)
  • Sort objects into groups of 4 and 5
  • Practice writing 4 and 5
  • Play "Five Little Ducks" counting game
Friday: Review 1-5
  • Number hunt: Find numbers 1-5 around the house
  • Count snacks and eat them (1-5 crackers)
  • Write numbers 1-5 from memory
  • Play number matching game

Science & Discovery

30 min/day

Learning Objectives

  • Observe and describe the natural world
  • Understand living vs. non-living things
  • Learn about weather and seasons
  • Explore the five senses
  • Conduct simple experiments and make predictions
  • Learn about plants, animals, and their habitats

Resources Used

PBS Kids - Sid the Science Kid National Geographic Kids Nature Walks Experiment Guides Science Journals

Sample Week: Exploring the Five Senses (FREE PREVIEW)

Monday: Sense of Touch
  • Create a "feely box" with different textures
  • Sort objects: soft vs. hard, smooth vs. rough
  • Go on a texture walk outside
  • Draw favorite textures in science journal
Tuesday: Sense of Sight
  • Color mixing experiment (red + blue = purple)
  • I Spy game focusing on colors and shapes
  • Watch Sid the Science Kid: "Sense of Sight"
  • Draw what you see out the window
Wednesday: Sense of Hearing
  • Sound scavenger hunt (find 5 different sounds)
  • Make simple instruments (rice shakers, drum)
  • Loud vs. quiet sorting game
  • Listen to nature sounds and identify them
Thursday: Sense of Taste & Smell
  • Taste test: sweet, salty, sour, bitter
  • Smell jars: identify scents (vanilla, lemon, cinnamon)
  • Make a simple snack and describe tastes/smells
  • Discuss how taste and smell work together
Friday: All Five Senses Together
  • Nature walk using all five senses
  • Create a "Five Senses" poster
  • Play "Which Sense?" guessing game
  • Celebrate with sensory play (playdough, water beads)

Social Studies & Good Citizenship

20 min/day

Learning Objectives

  • Understand family roles and relationships
  • Learn about community helpers (firefighters, doctors, teachers)
  • Recognize basic rules and why they matter
  • Identify feelings and practice empathy
  • Learn about different cultures and traditions
  • Understand basic geography (home, neighborhood, town)

Good Citizenship Skills

  • Following rules at home and in public
  • Taking turns and sharing
  • Being kind and helpful to others
  • Respecting property and belongings
  • Using good manners (please, thank you)
  • Being a good friend and classmate
  • Helping to keep spaces clean and tidy
Texas Requirement: Good Citizenship is one of the five required subjects for Texas homeschoolers. Our kindergarten curriculum teaches foundational character traits and social responsibility.

Resources Used

PBS Kids - Daniel Tiger Library Books Character Education Lessons Social Stories Activity Guides

Creative Arts & Music

30 min/day

Learning Objectives

  • Explore different art materials (crayons, paint, clay)
  • Learn basic colors and color mixing
  • Practice cutting, gluing, and fine motor skills
  • Sing songs and learn simple rhythms
  • Express creativity through open-ended projects
  • Learn about famous artists and their styles

Resources Used

Art for Kids Hub (YouTube) Music Together Songs Art Project Guides Printable Templates

Physical Education & Movement

30 min/day

Learning Objectives

  • Develop gross motor skills (running, jumping, hopping)
  • Practice balance and coordination
  • Learn to throw and catch
  • Follow movement instructions and games
  • Understand the importance of exercise and healthy eating

Resources Used

GoNoodle Videos Outdoor Play Movement Activity Cards

Sample Daily Schedule

A typical kindergarten day balances structured learning with plenty of play and movement breaks.

9:00 - 9:15

Morning Circle Time

Calendar, weather, songs, and daily plan review

9:15 - 10:00

Reading & Phonics

Letter learning, phonics practice, read-aloud story

10:00 - 10:15

Snack & Movement Break

Healthy snack, GoNoodle video, or outdoor play

10:15 - 10:45

Mathematics

Counting, number recognition, hands-on math activities

10:45 - 11:15

Science or Social Studies

Experiments, nature observation, or community learning

11:15 - 11:45

Creative Arts

Drawing, painting, crafts, or music activities

11:45 - 12:00

Story Time & Wrap-Up

Read favorite book, review what we learned, free play

Flexible Schedule: This is a suggested schedule. Feel free to adjust based on your child's energy levels and interests. Many families split the day with academics in the morning and creative/physical activities in the afternoon.

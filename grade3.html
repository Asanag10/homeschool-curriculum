What Your 3rd Grader Will Learn

Third grade is a pivotal year where students transition from "learning to read" to "reading to learn." They'll master multiplication and division, write multi-paragraph stories, and explore the world through science and social studies.

Complete Subject Breakdown

Mathematics

60 min/day

Learning Objectives

  • Master multiplication tables (0-12)
  • Understand division concepts and facts
  • Work with fractions (halves, thirds, fourths)
  • Measure length, weight, and volume
  • Tell time to the nearest minute
  • Solve multi-step word problems
  • Understand area and perimeter
  • Work with money (making change)

Reading & Literature

45 min/day

Learning Objectives

  • Read fluently at 100+ words per minute
  • Identify main idea and supporting details
  • Make inferences from text
  • Compare and contrast characters/events
  • Understand cause and effect
  • Build vocabulary (1000+ new words)
  • Read chapter books independently
  • Distinguish fact from opinion

Recommended Reading List

📚 Charlotte's Web by E.B. White
📚 The Magic Tree House series
📚 Tales of a Fourth Grade Nothing
📚 The BFG by Roald Dahl

Writing & Grammar

45 min/day

Learning Objectives

  • Write multi-paragraph narratives
  • Organize ideas with topic sentences
  • Use descriptive language and details
  • Master basic punctuation and capitalization
  • Identify and use parts of speech
  • Write opinion pieces with reasons
  • Edit and revise own writing
  • Use complete sentences consistently

Writing Projects Include

✍️ Personal narratives
✍️ Book reports
✍️ How-to guides
✍️ Poetry (haiku, acrostic)
✍️ Letter writing

Spelling

20 min/day

Learning Objectives

  • Master 500+ grade-level spelling words
  • Learn common spelling patterns and rules
  • Understand prefixes, suffixes, and root words
  • Apply spelling rules in writing
  • Use spelling strategies (sound it out, word families)
  • Build vocabulary through spelling study

Weekly Spelling Pattern Examples

📝 Long vowel patterns (ai, ay, ee, ea)
📝 R-controlled vowels (ar, er, ir, or, ur)
📝 Silent letters (kn, wr, mb)
📝 Compound words
📝 Homophones (their/there/they're)
Texas Requirement: Spelling is one of the five required subjects for Texas homeschoolers. Our curriculum includes weekly spelling lists, practice activities, and assessments.

Science

45 min/day

Units Covered

  • Life Science: Plant & animal life cycles, habitats, adaptations
  • Earth Science: Weather, climate, rocks & minerals, water cycle
  • Physical Science: Matter, energy, force & motion, simple machines
  • Scientific Method: Observations, hypotheses, experiments

Hands-On Experiments

🔬 Growing plants from seeds

Social Studies & Good Citizenship

30 min/day

Topics Explored

  • Communities and how they work
  • Map skills (compass rose, legends, scale)
  • Geography of the United States
  • Native American cultures
  • Government basics (local, state, federal)
  • Economics (goods, services, supply & demand)
  • Cultural diversity and traditions
  • Historical thinking and timelines

Good Citizenship Skills

  • Understanding rights and responsibilities
  • Respecting rules and authority
  • Being a helpful community member
  • Showing respect for others and their property
  • Practicing honesty and integrity
  • Contributing to the common good
  • Understanding civic participation
Texas Requirement: Good Citizenship is one of the five required subjects for Texas homeschoolers. Our curriculum integrates character education and civic responsibility throughout social studies lessons.

Projects & Activities

🗺️ Create a community map

First Grade Explorers!

What Your First Grader Will Learn

First grade builds on kindergarten foundations with more advanced reading, writing, and math skills. Students develop independence while exploring new subjects with curiosity and confidence.

Complete Subject Breakdown

Reading & Phonics

45 min/day

Learning Objectives

  • Read simple books independently with fluency
  • Master phonics patterns (blends, digraphs, long vowels)
  • Build sight word vocabulary (100+ high-frequency words)
  • Understand story elements (characters, setting, plot)
  • Answer comprehension questions about texts
  • Make predictions and connections while reading

Resources Used

Khan Academy Reading Starfall Learn to Read Library Early Readers Phonics Worksheets Decodable Readers

Sample Week 1: Short Vowel Review (FREE PREVIEW)

Monday: Short A Review
  • Review short A sound with word families (-at, -an, -ap)
  • Read "The Cat Sat" decodable book
  • Practice sight words: the, and, a, to
  • Complete word sort activity (short A vs. other vowels)
Tuesday: Short E & I Sounds
  • Learn short E and I word families (-et, -en, -it, -in)
  • Read "Ten Hens" (short E focus)
  • Build CVC words with letter tiles
  • Khan Academy phonics practice
Wednesday: Short O & U Sounds
  • Practice short O and U word families (-ot, -op, -ug, -un)
  • Read "The Bug in the Jug"
  • Write sentences using CVC words
  • Sight word flashcard review
Thursday: Mixed Vowel Practice
  • Read story with all short vowels
  • Complete comprehension questions
  • Play vowel sound bingo game
  • Independent reading time (15 min)
Friday: Assessment & Reading Fun
  • Short vowel assessment (informal)
  • Partner reading with family member
  • Visit library or read favorite book
  • Celebrate week's progress!

Spelling & Word Study

20 min/day

Learning Objectives

  • Spell CVC words and simple word families correctly
  • Master 100+ sight word spellings
  • Understand basic spelling patterns and rules
  • Use invented spelling for unknown words
  • Build word families and recognize patterns
  • Apply spelling knowledge in writing

Weekly Word Lists Include

📝 CVC words (cat, dog, sun, bed, hop)
📝 Sight words (said, have, like, come, some)
📝 Word families (-all, -ill, -ell)
📝 Simple blends (st, bl, cr, tr)
Texas Requirement: Spelling is one of the five required subjects for Texas homeschoolers. Our 1st grade curriculum builds on phonics foundations with systematic spelling instruction.

Resources Used

Spelling City Games Weekly Spelling Lists Practice Worksheets

Writing & Grammar

30 min/day

Learning Objectives

  • Write complete sentences with proper capitalization
  • Use periods, question marks, and exclamation points
  • Write simple stories with beginning, middle, and end
  • Form uppercase and lowercase letters correctly
  • Use descriptive words in writing
  • Write opinion pieces (I like... because...)

Writing Projects Include

✍️ Personal narratives (My favorite day)
✍️ How-to writing (How to make a sandwich)
✍️ Opinion writing (My favorite animal)
✍️ Friendly letters
Premium Feature: Our original writing curriculum includes step-by-step lessons, writing prompts, and grading rubrics designed specifically for 1st graders.

Resources Used

Original Writing Curriculum ReadWriteThink Activities Handwriting Practice Pages

Mathematics

45 min/day

Learning Objectives

  • Add and subtract within 20 fluently
  • Understand place value (tens and ones)
  • Count to 120 starting from any number
  • Tell time to the hour and half hour
  • Measure length using non-standard and standard units
  • Identify and create patterns
  • Understand greater than, less than, equal to
  • Solve word problems using addition and subtraction

Resources Used

Khan Academy - 1st Grade Math Math Playground Games Household Objects for Counting Math Worksheets Manipulative Guides

Sample Week: Addition Within 10 (FREE PREVIEW)

Monday: Introduction to Addition
  • Use objects to show combining groups (3 + 2 = 5)
  • Learn the + and = symbols
  • Khan Academy: Addition basics
  • Practice with counters or toys
Tuesday: Addition Facts to 5
  • Practice all combinations that make 5
  • Use fingers to add
  • Play addition card games
  • Complete addition worksheet
Wednesday: Addition Facts to 10
  • Practice combinations that make 10
  • Use ten frames for visual support
  • Math Playground addition games
  • Real-world addition problems
Thursday: Word Problems
  • Solve simple addition word problems
  • Draw pictures to represent problems
  • Write own addition stories
  • Practice mental math
Friday: Review & Games
  • Addition fact practice (timed for fun)
  • Play addition board game
  • Real-life addition (counting snacks)
  • Celebrate progress!

Science

30 min/day

Units Covered

  • Plants and their life cycles
  • Animals and their habitats
  • Weather and seasons
  • Matter: solids, liquids, and gases
  • The five senses (review and expand)
  • Simple machines and forces
  • Earth and sky observations

Resources Used

PBS Kids - Sid the Science Kid National Geographic Kids Nature Walks & Observations Experiment Guides Science Journals

Social Studies & Good Citizenship

30 min/day

Learning Objectives

  • Learn about community helpers and their roles
  • Study basic map skills (symbols, directions)
  • Explore American symbols and holidays
  • Understand rules, laws, and why they matter
  • Learn about different cultures and traditions
  • Study family history and timelines

Good Citizenship Skills

  • Being a responsible family and community member
  • Showing respect for others and property
  • Following rules and understanding consequences
  • Helping others and being kind
  • Taking care of the environment
Texas Requirement: Good Citizenship is one of the five required subjects for Texas homeschoolers. Our curriculum teaches character development and civic responsibility.

Resources Used

Library Books on Communities Social Studies Lessons Map Activities

Arts & Physical Education

30 min/day

Learning Objectives

  • Create art using various materials and techniques
  • Learn about famous artists and art styles
  • Sing songs and learn basic music concepts
  • Develop gross motor skills through active play
  • Practice coordination and balance
  • Understand importance of physical fitness

Resources Used

Sample Daily Schedule

A typical 1st grade day balances academics with creative activities and movement breaks.

9:00 - 9:45

Reading & Phonics

Phonics lesson, guided reading, independent practice

9:45 - 10:15

Writing & Spelling

Handwriting, sentence writing, spelling practice

10:15 - 10:30

Snack & Movement Break

Healthy snack, GoNoodle, or outdoor play

10:30 - 11:15

Mathematics

Math lesson, hands-on activities, practice problems

11:15 - 12:00

Lunch & Free Play

Lunch break and unstructured play time

12:00 - 12:30

Science or Social Studies

Alternating days, experiments and exploration

12:30 - 1:00

Arts & Enrichment

Art projects, music, or physical education

Flexible Schedule: This is a suggested schedule. Adjust based on your child's needs and family routine. Many families do academics in the morning and save creative activities for afternoon.
